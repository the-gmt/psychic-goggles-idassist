Understanding Your Report

Your credit report is a detailed record of your borrowing history. Here's what to know.

1

Review it regularly. Check for accounts you don't recognize — these can be an early sign of identity fraud.

2

Check for errors. Even minor mistakes — wrong address, incorrect account status — can be disputed and corrected for free.

3

Closed accounts still matter. Positive closed accounts continue helping your history length for up to 10 years.
