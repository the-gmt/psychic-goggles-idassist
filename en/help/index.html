Scam Protection

ID Assist Scan & Score is an advanced AI-powered tool that helps you identify scams before they can cause harm. Submit any suspicious email, text message, image, or website link and our technology instantly analyzes it and provides a Scam Score that tells you the level of risk. Each result comes with specific, actionable steps you can take to protect yourself.

AutoScan continuously monitors your primary Gmail or personal Outlook inbox 24/7 using the same AI technology that powers manual Scan & Score. When a potential threat is detected, you'll receive an immediate alert with the Scam Score and recommended steps – before you've even opened the email.

Emails received in secondary inboxes, folders, or categories outside your primary inbox need to be manually submitted for scanning.

To enable AutoScan

Log in to your account and go to Scan & Score AutoScan in your profile menu. Follow the prompts to connect your Gmail or personal Outlook account.

For security and privacy reasons, AutoScan currently supports personal Gmail and personal Outlook accounts only. Work and organizational accounts often have security restrictions and administrative controls that prevent third-party services from connecting.

You can still scan suspicious work emails using the manual Scan & Score feature – just copy and paste the message content for the same AI-powered analysis.

You can submit four types of content for analysis:

  • Emails – copy and paste the complete message, including the sender's name and email address
  • Text messages – paste the message content along with the sender's phone number
  • Images and screenshots – upload a photo or screenshot of any suspicious communication
  • URLs – submit any website address you want to verify before visiting

Note that images or photos that don't contain readable text or a QR code cannot be analyzed at this time.

Every submission is assigned one of three risk levels:

  • Low Risk – The communication appears to come from a legitimate source and contains the typical characteristics of genuine communications.
  • High Risk – Multiple indicators of deceptive intent have been detected. The communication poses a significant risk of fraud or identity theft.
  • Risk Level Inconclusive – The submitted content may be incomplete or doesn't contain enough information for a full analysis. Try resubmitting with complete message content.

Each result includes contributing factors that explain why the score was assigned, along with specific recommended next steps.

The more information you include, the more accurate the analysis will be. For best results:

  • Emails and texts: Include the complete message, sender details, and any links or URLs
  • Images: Make sure the full message is visible, including any text, QR codes, sender name, and dates
  • URLs: Submit the complete website address – a screenshot of a URL cannot be analyzed

Credit Monitoring

Depending on your plan, ID Assist monitors your credit files at both Equifax and TransUnion on a daily basis. You'll be alerted if changes appear that could be a sign of identity theft – such as a new credit inquiry or account – so you can take action before any damage is done.

You can also request an updated credit score from Equifax at any time, with a fresh score available every 30 days. An updated credit report is available to request every three months, giving you a detailed snapshot of your credit history and any recent changes.

Canada has two major credit bureaus – Equifax and TransUnion. Lenders don't always report to the same one, and a fraudster applying for credit in your name might only trigger an inquiry at one bureau. If you're only monitoring one, that activity can go undetected until the damage is done.

ID Assist Platinum and Ultimate monitor both bureaus every day – so you're notified at the earliest possible sign of fraudulent activity, regardless of which bureau it appears at first.

No. ID Assist uses a soft inquiry when monitoring your credit file, which has no impact on your credit score or report. Soft inquiries include your own requests for your credit report, promotional inquiries from lenders, and checkup inquiries from your existing creditors.

Only hard inquiries – made by lenders when you apply for new credit – can affect your score.

Log in to your ID Assist account and go to Credit Scores & Reports on your dashboard.

  • To view your credit score, click Request Credit Score. A new score is available to request every 30 days.
  • To view your credit report, click View Equifax Report. An updated report is available to request every three months.

Note that you'll need to complete your credit bureau authentication questions before your report or score can be displayed.

Contact the credit bureaus directly to dispute any inaccuracies:

Equifax Canada – Submit a dispute online

TransUnion Canada – Submit a dispute online

Equifax will investigate and can take up to 20 business days to respond. TransUnion's self-service site allows you to view your credit file and submit disputes directly.

A credit score is a three-digit number (typically 300–900) calculated from your credit report. A higher score signals better creditworthiness to lenders, which can improve your eligibility for loans, mortgages, and credit cards – and reduce the interest rates you're offered.

An unexpected drop in your score or a new inquiry you don't recognize can be one of the first signs of identity fraud.

Dark Web Monitoring

The dark web consists of anonymous online networks used for buying and selling illicit goods – including stolen personal information, credit card numbers, Social Insurance Numbers, and other credentials that criminals use to commit identity fraud.

ID Assist uses multiple data providers to continuously search criminal marketplaces for the personal information you've registered with us. These searches use both automated tools and human searchers to find exposed information as quickly as possible. When any of your registered information is detected, we alert you immediately so you can take action – like changing passwords or cancelling a compromised card.

You can register up to 50 items across 12 categories, including:

  • Up to 10 credit and debit card numbers
  • Up to 10 bank account numbers
  • Up to 5 email addresses
  • Up to 5 phone numbers
  • Up to 10 loyalty card numbers
  • Up to 5 health benefit plan numbers
  • Social Insurance Number (SIN)
  • Driver's licence
  • Up to 2 passports
  • Permanent Resident Card number

The more information you register, the broader your protection. We recommend registering all items that apply to you.

Log in to your account to review the full alert details. Depending on what was found, recommended steps may include changing passwords, contacting your bank or card issuer, or placing a fraud alert on your credit files. If you need help interpreting an alert or aren't sure what to do, call our Contact Centre – our team can guide you through the right response.

Common warning signs include:

  • Accounts, inquiries, or addresses you don't recognize appearing on your credit report
  • Bills or collection calls for accounts you didn't open
  • Expected mail or statements that stop arriving
  • Being denied credit unexpectedly
  • Unexpected activity in your CRA My Account (e.g., returns filed or direct deposit information changed)
  • A dark web monitoring alert from ID Assist

If you notice any of these, act quickly – contact your financial institutions and call our team.

ID Assist Platinum and Ultimate include Full-Service Restoration, which means our specialists are with you every step of the way through the recovery process. This includes:

  • Placing fraud alerts at both Equifax and TransUnion
  • Contacting banks, creditors, and credit bureaus
  • Drafting and sending dispute letters
  • Following up until each fraudulent item is resolved
  • Cheque reimbursement if a fraudulent cheque is drawn on your account1
  • Emergency cash advance if your account is compromised while you're travelling2

Password Manager

ID Assist Password Manager is a secure tool included with the Ultimate plan that helps you create, store, and manage strong, unique passwords for all your online accounts. Using browser extensions and the mobile app, you can log in to any website in seconds without having to remember every password.

Industry-leading encryption keeps your credentials safe, and two-factor authentication adds an additional layer of security to your Password Manager account itself.

Weak or reused passwords are one of the most common ways criminals gain access to online accounts. A password manager makes it easy to use strong, unique passwords for every account – so that if one account is compromised, your others remain safe.

Visit our dedicated Password Manager help centre at landing.idassist.com/passwordhelp for step-by-step guides on getting started, managing your passwords, and using browser extensions. You can also contact our team directly for additional assistance.
